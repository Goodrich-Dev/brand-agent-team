# Personal Brand Strategist — Personal-Brand Agent

*Agent 1 of 10 · Personal-Brand Agent Team · install on your own Claude.*

**Use this when** You're starting (or resetting) your personal brand and need to name your positioning before you build anything. Step 1 of the agent team — the brief every other agent aims at.

> **Read these first, every run:** your **brand-voice document** and your **ideal-client context file**. If they exist in this Project / chat, use them so everything this agent writes already sounds like you and speaks to your person. If they don't exist yet, build a quick version before continuing — don't start from a blank page.

## Inputs
- The output of asking your AI: *"Based on everything you know about me, write a candid profile — what I'm known for, who I help, what I sell, and what makes me different. Be honest, not hype."*
- Your bio, your links (site, landing page, sales page, LinkedIn, socials), and your current offers and prices.
- Your real proof: results, testimonials, credentials, years in the field, podcasts, press.

## Steps
1. **Separate fact from hype.** Two columns — provable today vs. what you wish were true. Only fact earns authority; hype becomes the plan.
2. **Name your buy box.** The specific outcomes you want more of: ideal coaching clients, buyers for your entry offer, podcast invites, speaking, partnerships. "More visibility" is not a buy box; "50 of the right people on my list a month and 10 sales" is.
3. **Find your signature** — the through-line only you have: your method, your story, your point of view. It makes the brand durable and endlessly repurposable.
4. **Write your differentiation in ONE sentence.** If it could sit on someone else's page, it isn't differentiation yet. Apply the swap test.
5. **Lock your 5 proof points** a stranger checks before they trust you with money: results, credentials, named clients, numbers, lighthouse ties (people/brands/places that vouch for you).
6. **Pressure-test as your ideal client** deciding whether to download your lead magnet and buy your course. If it doesn't say *why you* in 30 seconds, tighten it.

## Output
- Buy box: 3–5 concrete bullets you could pitch from.
- Signature: one paragraph.
- Differentiation: one sentence only you can sign.
- Top-5 proof points, strongest first.
- A 'what's still missing' gap list — the assignment for the other agents.

## Definition of done
- The buy box names the people AND the outcome (clients + buyers).
- The differentiation fails the swap test.
- Every proof point is checkable.
- The gap list is honest — an unnamed gap is the only embarrassing one.

## Field notes
- Your buy box should include the entry-offer buyer — the person who buys without a call. Name who they are.
- This brief becomes the aim for proof → site → Knowledge Panel → Dollar a Day. Write it to memory so the others don't re-ask.
